How BITESHA works

A smart contract enforces the split and records everything. People, receipts and independent oversight handle the real-world step.

You contribute USDC

Stablecoin keeps the food budget steady — $80 earmarked for food stays worth $80 until the food is bought.

The contract splits 80/20 — immutably

80% to a food fund, 20% to operations. The ratio is fixed in code; not even the team or the DAO can change it. Rounding always favours food (provably ≥ 80%).

Food is bought for NYC streets

Operators withdraw from the food fund to buy meals from real suppliers. Each spend logs a purpose and a receipt hash on-chain.

You get BTSH — a receipt + a vote

A donation receipt and a governance vote in the BITESHA DAO. No yield, no return — a voice, not an investment.

Monthly signed reports, anchored on-chain

Receipts, photos and video are bundled, cryptographically signed, and anchored on-chain so anyone can verify them.

What we’re honest about

The smart contract guarantees the split and the transparency. It cannot, by itself, guarantee that the 80% is physically spent on food — that final step happens in the real world, secured by published receipts, photos, video, and multisig control including independent representatives, not by code. We say this plainly because a charity that overstates its technology isn’t trustworthy.
